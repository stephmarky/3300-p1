What makes a country happy?

Citizen happiness is used to justify everything from wide-sweeping policies to regime changes. It is the backbone of political campaigns, and a topic constantly up for contentious debate. Understanding the source of happiness is crucial to ensuring a country’s prosperity and its citizens’ wellbeing. In our research project, we aimed to tackle these questions for developed European OECD countries by finding surprising correlations between various variables and the happiness of each nation (measured by the 2017 World Happiness Report).

Government Health Expenditure per Person

A healthy populace makes for a happy one. Governments that invest in the health of their citizens tend to have happier populations.

"It is health that is real wealth not pieces of gold and silver." –Mahatma Gandhi

Source: GapMinder

Bureaucratic Honesty

The citizens care about the integrity of their political systems. Regime honesty is well-correlated with citizen happiness.

"Honesty is the best policy." –Benjamin Franklin

Source: Transparency Int'l

College Education of Citizens Ages 55-64

Perhaps the happiness of today’s society rests upon the education of our predecessors.

"Education is the premise of progress, in every society, in every family." –Kofi Annan

Source: OECD Data, OECD.org

Antidepressant Use per 1000 Citizens

Actually happy, or just well-medicated? Perhaps the contentment of countries is manufactured through medication.

"Instead of removing the conditions that make people depressed modern society gives them antidepressant drugs." –Theodore J. Kaczynski

Source: OECD iLibrary, NCBI
